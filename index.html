I came to LSA to be in a creative field whilst also having freedom in my life. I wouldn’t say that i had a hard time in secondary, rules wise, but I do feel that i would thrive a lot more in an environment that wasn’t so restrictive. LSA became a good option for me, as it is a college you are granted a lot more rights in terms of opinions and decisions. Some other less important reasons were the set design course looked great and very well funded and the school presented itself with many good opportunities, even opportunities after you leave the school. Some things i have learned include; story telling, camera for craft, industry progression and art direction. I would say out of all of those camera for craft was the most memorable as my teacher was absolutely amazing but overall i have learned the most industry skills and practices in art direction. I learned things like practitioners and skills that will make me look professional in the industry when it comes to being able to sound knowledgable. I would actually say that my classes and experience have changed my future goals, especially having the opportunity to work on set as a runner, now i would say I’m leaning towards set design and making my way into it by beating a runner or trainee and climbing the ropes from the bottom upwards whereas before i would have preferred something more secure. My end goal right now is to be an art director or prop master but i think i will be happy as long as i am doing something art based. A reflection from last year i have would be i over analysed everything, slightly too much if thats possible. I rambled on and on in reflections when a lot of what i was saying was just repetitions and just saying things that didn’t need to be said making everything quite draining to read. I would like to develop my ability to write accurately and write in an intelligent and entertaining manner. I would also like to make a meaningful FMP, i felt last year my project ended up not really aligning with my beliefs and i feel i could have done something that made an impact. Perhaps something to do with my identity in areas i haven’t explored, such as my genetics that i have never got to learn about. I also want my FMP to develop my skills in storytelling and camera work. As craft students we don’t really get much experience in filming. To be honest, i wish we did as this is something i am also very interested in. It would also be helpful going into the industry if i had prior knowledge on what the teams around me are doing, just to have an overview. I know I definitely want a distinction grade in the summer, i got one last FMP and i do plan to get one again. I will ensure this by trying as hard as i can. I am going to work overtime and put all my effort in after all this is what we have been working towards for the last two years. This FMP process will help prepare us for our next steps after LSA as i will know and have experience ideating ideas from a pre prepared brief, then taking those ideas through development stages, getting constructive criticism and support from peers then, actually producing your ideas and exhibiting it all is just true experience and good practice for the future. My professional behaviours will be developed by this project in terms of ideation, i would say that I’m pretty good at most things we will have to do in our FMPs , fr example: analysing, reflecting and making, but one part i find quite hard is ideating. I tend to stick to an idea from the start and not be able to move past it to find better ideas. This is definitely something i want to move past as i may be missing cool opportunities of things to make by doing this. I also hope to create something and then make a short film out of it so it’s a well rounded project. This will help my career goals and aspirations because if it comes to applying for jobs or opportunities and i have a project that shows i can do all parts of developing a project to a high standard i will come off as a strong candidate for any job, showing knowledge and experience. I do also hope my FMP is specific to my skill set. I have a wide range of skills and can pick up anything pretty quickly so i want to make something that is different. I know most ideas have been done before but i aim to use my personality and creative flair to make something that stands out from the crowd.
